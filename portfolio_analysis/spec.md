# Daniel Huie Portfolio Specification & Analysis

## 1. Overview
**URL:** [https://danielhuie.com](https://danielhuie.com)

**Current State:**
The website is a clean, photography-heavy portfolio built on Squarespace. It currently serves a broad range of clients, showcasing Corporate Events, Sports/Fitness, Weddings, Food, and Portraits.

**Goal:**
Redo/Modernize to be more **client-focused**, demonstrating **business value** and impact specifically for **fitness, tech, finance, and wellness** event clients.

---

## 2. Sitemap & Content Inventory
The site structure is flat with the following primary pages:

*   **Home (`/`)**: Landing page with a highlight reel (video) and featured categories.
*   **Corporate Events (`/corporate-events`)**: Gallery of corporate gatherings, panels, and networking events (e.g., NYSE, Singapore Global Network).
*   **Sports & Fitness Events (`/sports-fitness-events`)**: Gallery of races, fitness challenges, and brand activations (e.g., Vuori, Mush).
*   **Videos (`/videos`)**: embedded highlight reels.
*   **Weddings & Engagements (`/weddings-engagements`)**: Personal event photography.
*   **Food (`/food`)**: Culinary photography.
*   **Portraits (`/portraits`)**: Headshots and creative portraits.
*   **About (`/about`)**: Bio, philosophy ("ROI-Focused"), and client list.
*   **Contact (`/contact`)**: Simple inquiry form.

## 3. Visual Identity & Design System
The current design is minimal, letting the imagery take center stage.

### Typography
*   **Headings:** `futura-pt` (Geometric Sans-Serif)
    *   Usage: Uppercase, bold, modern.
*   **Body Text:** `proxima-nova` (Sans-Serif)
    *   Usage: Clean, highly readable.

### Color Palette
*   **Background:** `#FCFCFC` (`rgb(252, 252, 252)`) - Off-white/Paper white.
*   **Headings:** `#222222` (`rgb(34, 34, 34)`) - Soft Black.
*   **Body Text:** `#575757` (`rgb(87, 87, 87)`) - Dark Gray.

### Layout Patterns
*   **Navigation:** Left-aligned vertical sidebar on desktop (logo + links).
*   **Galleries:** Grid-based masonry layouts for images.
*   **Homepage:** Hero text/banner followed by category links/previews.

## 4. Content & Messaging Analysis

### Strengths
*   **ROI-Centric Messaging:** The *Home* and *About* pages already contain strong copy regarding business value:
    *   *"Your Events Deserve More Than Just Coverageâ€”They Deserve Results"*
    *   *"I create marketing assets that work as hard as you do."*
    *   *"ROI-Focused: Corporate Events That Pay for Themselves"*
*   **High-Quality Imagery:** The portfolio content is visually strong and professional.
*   **Client Logos/Names:** Mentions credible clients like *Vuori, Mush, Mount to Coast, Singapore Global Network, NYSE*.

### Weaknesses & Opportunities (Gap Analysis)
*   **Diluted Focus:** The inclusion of **Weddings**, **Food**, and generic **Portraits** in the main navigation dilutes the B2B/Commercial positioning. If the goal is to target Tech/Finance/Wellness brands, personal events (Weddings) should likely be removed or moved to a sub-menu/separate domain.
*   **Generic Grouping:**
    *   "Corporate Events" is a catch-all. It could be split or tagged to specifically highlight **Tech** and **Finance** sectors (e.g., "Tech Summits", "Finance & Markets").
    *   "Sports & Fitness" covers "Wellness", but "Wellness" isn't explicitly top-level, which is a desired target vertical.
*   **Show, Don't Just Tell (Business Value):** While the *text* claims ROI, the *galleries* are just grids of images. There are no case studies, testimonials, or data points (e.g., "This video helped sell out the 2024 conference").
*   **Tech & Finance Representation:** While "AI Global Leadership Summit @ NYSE" is a heading, the visual language doesn't immediately scream "Tech/Finance" distinct from general corporate.

## 5. Technical Stack
*   **Platform:** Squarespace (evident from CDN links `images.squarespace-cdn.com` and structure).
*   **Assets:**
    *   Images served via Squarespace CDN (auto-resized/formatted).
    *   Videos embedded (likely Vimeo/YouTube).
*   **Frontend:** Standard HTML/CSS generated by Squarespace templates.

## 6. Recommendations for Modernization
1.  **Restructure Navigation:**
    *   Prioritize: *Corporate (Tech/Finance)*, *Fitness & Wellness*, *Brand Activations*.
    *   Deprioritize/Hide: *Weddings*, *Food*.
2.  **Case Study Format:** Convert simple image grids into Case Studies that explain the *Brief*, the *Solution*, and the *Result* (Impact/Value).
3.  **Industry-Specific Landing Pages:** Create dedicated pages/sections for "Tech & Finance" and "Fitness & Wellness" to tailor the imagery and copy to those specific buyers.
4.  **Social Proof:** Add client testimonials specifically praising the business impact of the work.
